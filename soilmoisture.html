Soil Moisture Guide for Farmers

Soil moisture is crucial for healthy crops, measured from 0 (very dry) to 1 (fully saturated). Here's a quick guide to understand moisture levels and actions you can take.

Soil Moisture Levels

  • 0.0 - 0.1: Very Dry
  • 0.1 - 0.2: Low Moisture
  • 0.2 - 0.3: Just Right
  • 0.3 - 0.4: Too Much Water
  • 0.4 and above: Waterlogged

Action Steps

0.0 - 0.1: Very Dry

  • Water crops immediately.
  • Check irrigation system.
  • Add mulch to retain moisture.
  • Monitor weather for rain.

0.1 - 0.2: Low Moisture

  • Water if rain isn't expected.
  • Regularly check soil moisture.
  • Adjust watering based on crop needs.

0.2 - 0.3: Just Right

  • No additional watering needed.
  • Continue to monitor moisture levels.

0.3 - 0.4: Too Much Water

  • Reduce watering to prevent waterlogging.
  • Improve drainage if necessary.

0.4 and above: Waterlogged

  • Drain excess water quickly.
  • Avoid sensitive crops during wet conditions.
