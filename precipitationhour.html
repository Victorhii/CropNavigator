Understanding Precipitation for Farmers (mm/hr)

Precipitation, measured in mm/hr, is vital for managing irrigation and crop health. This guide outlines various precipitation levels and recommended actions.

Precipitation Levels

  • 0 - 1 mm/hr: Very Low (Water as needed)
  • 1 - 2 mm/hr: Low (Monitor crop needs)
  • 2 - 5 mm/hr: Moderate (Good for crops)
  • 5 - 10 mm/hr: High (Potential for waterlogging)
  • 10 mm/hr and above: Very High (Flood risk)

Action Steps

0 - 1 mm/hr: Very Low

  • Water crops as needed.
  • Regularly check soil moisture.

Consider water-efficient irrigation methods.

1 - 2 mm/hr: Low

  • Water crops based on needs.
  • Monitor weather forecasts.

2 - 5 mm/hr: Moderate

  • Assess soil moisture levels.
  • Check crop health regularly.

5 - 10 mm/hr: High

  • Ensure proper drainage systems.
  • Watch for signs of fungal diseases.

10 mm/hr and above: Very High

  • Evaluate fields for flooding risks.
  • Implement erosion control measures.
