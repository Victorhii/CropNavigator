Understanding Precipitation for Farmers

Precipitation, measured in mm/day, is crucial for crop growth. This guide outlines precipitation levels and essential actions to optimize your farming practices.

Precipitation Levels

  • 0 - 2 mm/day: Very Low (Water crops)
  • 2 - 5 mm/day: Low (Monitor water needs)
  • 5 - 10 mm/day: Moderate (Good for crops)
  • 10 - 20 mm/day: High (Prevent waterlogging)
  • 20 mm/day and above: Very High (Flood risk)

Action Steps

0 - 2 mm/day: Very Low

  • Water crops.
  • Check soil moisture regularly.

Use water-efficient systems.

2 - 5 mm/day: Low

  • Water as needed.
  • Utilize rainwater harvesting.

5 - 10 mm/day: Moderate

  • Monitor soil moisture.
  • Check crop health.

10 - 20 mm/day: High

  • Ensure drainage.
  • Check for diseases.

20 mm/day and above: Very High

  • Evaluate for flooding.
  • Implement erosion control.
