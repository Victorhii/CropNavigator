Understanding Air Quality Index for Agriculture

The AQI measures air quality, with higher values indicating more pollution. Poor air quality can affect plant health and growth. Here's a guide on the AQI scale (1 to 5) and recommended agricultural actions for plants.

AQI Levels

  • AQI 1: Excellent (0 - 50)
  • AQI 2: Good (51 - 100)
  • AQI 3: Moderate (101 - 150)
  • AQI 4: Poor (151 - 200)
  • AQI 5: Hazardous (201+)

Plant Actions Based on AQI Levels

AQI 1: Excellent (0 - 50)

Optimal conditions for all planting, irrigation, and harvesting activities.

AQI 2: Good (51 - 100)

Continue normal planting and irrigation practices; minimal risk for plants.

AQI 3: Moderate (101 - 150)

Monitor sensitive plants for stress; consider using covers to protect against pollution.

AQI 4: Poor (151 - 200)

Implement protective measures for vulnerable plants; limit watering to reduce stress.

AQI 5: Hazardous (201+)

Halt planting and outdoor activities; cover sensitive plants and consider indoor protection.
