Understanding Evapotranspiration for Farmers

Evapotranspiration (ET) is essential for determining your crops' water needs, combining evaporation and transpiration, measured in mm/day. This guide helps you interpret ET levels and take appropriate actions.

Evapotranspiration Levels

  • 0 - 1 mm/day: Very Low (Water needed)
  • 1 - 3 mm/day: Low (Watering may be necessary)
  • 3 - 5 mm/day: Optimal (Watering is on track)
  • 5 - 7 mm/day: High (Increase watering frequency)
  • 7 mm/day and above: Very High (Critical water need)

Action Steps Based on ET Levels

0 - 1 mm/day: Very Low

Actions:

  • Water crops to prevent drought stress.
  • Check soil moisture regularly.
  • Use mulch to conserve moisture.

Consider collecting rainwater for irrigation!

1 - 3 mm/day: Low

Actions:

  • Water according to crop needs.
  • Plan for rainfall using forecasts.
  • Adjust irrigation times to reduce evaporation.

3 - 5 mm/day: Optimal

Actions:

  • Maintain current watering practices.
  • Monitor ET levels regularly.

5 - 7 mm/day: High

Actions:

  • Increase watering frequency.
  • Check for signs of heat stress.

7 mm/day and above: Very High

Actions:

  • Ensure enough water supply.
  • Avoid planting sensitive crops during this period.
